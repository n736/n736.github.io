Language Is Music

Read aloud a piece of text, which may be this one, at a slow, natural pace. At each word, enunciate each and every consonant and vowel. Focus on how each letter sounds, listening to the transient sounds of each consonant, the pitch of each vowel, and the rhythm of each and every syllable. After you finish reading this piece of text, you will repeat the piece of over and over, slightly changing your pronunciation with one of two possible variations:

  • Emphasize the percussive-sounding consonants until your voice becomes nothing but a drum kit
  • Elongate the vowels until you are singing or humming a melody

Repeat the piece of text until there is no text to be heard, but only the music your vocal interpretation has created.

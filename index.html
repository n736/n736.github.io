Gong Controller

with Matthew Goodheart

An experimental design for a controller interface for controlling... well... anything! The current prototype includes 10 touch potentiometers connected via I2C to an Arduino Nano, able to transmit OpenSoundControl messages over a USB Serial connection. These potentiometers not only give visual feedback, but can programmatically have its position set, allowing for a complex, hands-free change between different scenes. Currently, a Max/MSP interface exists to easily allow hooking up these parameters to a fun sounding object like resonators~, perfect for controlling a gong with an attached transducer.

The physical design is currently being modeling for 3D printing for a final product.

The Signal Experiment

A glitchy, multi-channel composition revolved around the machine learning of a signal. Each networked performer draws waveforms, training a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) based on their inputs. As time progresses, the MLP learns each performer's signal identity and interpolates between them. The MLP output is played back through a buffer at a swept frequency from 0 to the sample rate, with lots of aliasing and self-modulation. The spectroscope of this signal is a work of art itself. And of course, there are fun little glitch noises every time you click send.

Language Is Music

Read aloud a piece of text, which may be this one, at a slow, natural pace. At each word, enunciate each and every consonant and vowel. Focus on how each letter sounds, listening to the transient sounds of each consonant, the pitch of each vowel, and the rhythm of each and every syllable. After you finish reading this piece of text, you will repeat the piece of over and over, slightly changing your pronunciation with one of two possible variations:

  • Emphasize the percussive-sounding consonants until your voice becomes nothing but a drum kit
  • Elongate the vowels until you are singing or humming a melody

Repeat the piece of text until there is no text to be heard, but only the music your vocal interpretation has created.

An instruction score that sonically investigates the underlying musical elements of linguistics. Inspired by "I Am Sitting In A Room" by Alvin Lucier, the piece slowly transforms a simple piece of text into a form of pure sound. This piece has been designed with the composer's newly achieved understanding of sound, through Pauline Olivero's practice of deep listening. The score is featured in "A Year of Deep Listening" arranged by Stephanie Loveless.

Web Guitar

Zoom in/out if the content is too small/large.

A fun, guitar designed to run in the browser. A combination of data visualization techniques in JavaScript and an understanding of music, MIDI, and guitars. You can click each fret to play a melody or try chord mode, selecting each note to be part of a harmony. Or if you really just want to have fun, go to town in strum mode. Fun fact: I actually prefer to tune my guitar in all fourths from Eb; it just feels right.

Winter Rose

A heartwarming, Christmas-time composition that shines light on a beautiful flower, unusually existing in a snowy environment. The composition style is like a multi-dimensional see-saw, bouncing between contrasting thoughts. The exploitation varies across musical constructs: major against minor, regular against irregular time, and loud against soft. Although the piece sounds uniquely satisfying, it is quite difficult to play as is, leaving room to reinterpretation through different arrangements and orchestrations.
